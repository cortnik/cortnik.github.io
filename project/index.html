Women + Riyadh Metro

How the Other Half Travels: An analysis of university

women's mobility in Saudi Arabia's capital city

Background

In a city where women cannot drive, a public rail and bus system could have a tremendous impact on the mobility of women.

We are interested in studying the movements of women in Riyadh, and analyzing how the proposed rail system will allow them to move.

We would also like to identify areas where additional bus/ shuttle services could complement the rail system.

Methodology: Part One

Set focal point at the Princess Nourah Bint Abdul Rahman University Map CDR flows (based on the OD matrix) from cell towers around the University at 3 time slices on a typical school day:
9am - 12 noon - 6 pm

These would be brief snapshots of how ~ 60,000 female university students travel

Methodology: Part Two

Map Tweets by female users (either by user id info, or those who tweeted at Princess Noura University), tracing sequence of tweet locations as a proxy for travel flow.
These findings can be compared against Part One
