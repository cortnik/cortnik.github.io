WOMEN ON THE MOVE


In Saudi Arabia, women are not allowed to drive. Instead they rely on personal drivers to get around the city. A public transport system could have a tremendous impact on the mobility of women. Riyadh, Saudi Arabia's capital city, is currently building an extensive metro system, scheduled to be completed in 2018. The Women on the Move project seeks to understand the movements of women in Riyadh, and analyze how the proposed rail system serves the places they travel. Women on the Move created a sample population utilizing cell phone call detail records with origins and destinations at the all-women's Princess Noura University. This analysis identifies "hotspots" where women travel before or after going to the Princess Noura campus, as well as potential paths through the city. Finally, Women on the Move identifies the hotspots in Riyadh that are not served by the prposed rail system. Follow the link to the right to interact with the analysis.
